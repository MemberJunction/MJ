Education

The certifications employers and judges trust.

Four certification tracks. 50+ continuing-education courses. 47 culinary programs licensed to teach our curriculum. The credential that means something — because the federation that issues it is run by working cheesemakers.

Certifications

Four tracks · One craft

413 candidates currently enrolled
Foundation

Apprentice Cheesemaker

12 months · 4 courses · 80 mentor hours · $295 enrollment

247 currently enrolled

For people new to cheesemaking. Covers fundamentals: milk science, basic cultures, beginner techniques, safety. Pairs you with a mentor producer.

Track details
Mid-level

Certified Specialist (by style)

18 months · 6 courses · 120 hours · per-style enrollment

88 currently enrolled

Specialist tracks: Washed-Rind, Bloomy-Rind, Hard-Aged, Alpine, Blue, Fresh/Pasta Filata, Sheep & Goat. Deep practice in one style with a recognized specialist instructor.

7 specializations
Capstone · 3 years

Master Cheesemaker

3 years · 6 courses · 200 supervised hours · practical exam · $1,450

62 currently in program

The capstone. Requires Specialist completion in at least one style, 5+ years working in production, and 200 supervised hours making cheese under a sitting Master. Final exam includes a blind sensory panel and a live make.

Program details
Teaching

Certified Educator

12 months · 4 courses · teaching practicum · $495

16 currently enrolled

For culinary instructors, monger-trainers, and academics who want to teach the ICF curriculum at an Institutional-member program. Requires Master Cheesemaker OR equivalent industry credential.
