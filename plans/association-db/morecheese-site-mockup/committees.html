The work gets done in committees.

The Board sets direction. The five committee types — Board, Standing, Standards Working Groups, Ad Hoc, and Workgroup — do the actual work. Charters are public. Meetings are scheduled. Motions are recorded. Votes are tallied. Minutes are published. Open seats are listed openly so members know exactly how to get involved.

How federation committees work

Chartered. Member-led. Accountable.

Every committee has a published charter, an elected chair, defined member roles (Chair, Vice Chair, Secretary, Member, Non-Voting Member, Liaison, Advisor), and a recorded process. Motions are made. Votes are tallied. Minutes are drafted, reviewed, approved, and published. Action items are owned and tracked. Nothing about how the federation operates is opaque.
