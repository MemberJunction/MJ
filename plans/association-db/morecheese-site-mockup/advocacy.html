Advocacy

Where the federation stands. Why it matters.

Twelve active positions across US, EU, UK, Canada, Australia, and Japan jurisdictions. 47 advocacy actions filed in the past 12 months. Every position is researched, member-reviewed, and formally adopted by the Advocacy Committee before publication.

How we work

Research. Position. File. Mobilize.

Every advocacy position is the output of a defined process. Member-producers and the Advocacy Committee identify legislation or regulation that materially affects cheesemakers. Policy researchers and outside counsel produce a position paper. The committee debates, revises, and votes. Once adopted, the federation files formally — and mobilizes members to file individual comments when appropriate.

We do not lobby for individual businesses or interests. We file on questions of standards, safety, labeling, designation-of-origin, raw-milk regulation, small-producer accommodation, and trade. We are non-partisan. We have filed comments under every US administration since 1996.

United States · FDA

FDA Dairy Labeling Proposal (Docket FDA-2026-N-0418)

Support with Revisions

The federation supports clearer terroir-of-origin labeling for traditional cheeses but pushes back on the proposed pasteurization-disclosure language as overly broad and likely to confuse consumers about cheeses that have been safely produced from raw milk under existing FDA aging requirements. Position paper co-authored by the Standards Committee Labeling Working Group, chaired by Robert Kihm.

European Union · DG SANTE

EU Raw-Milk Cheese Carve-Out Proposal (EFSA 2026/0231)

Support

The federation strongly supports the Italian-French joint proposal to formally protect traditional raw-milk cheeses from the proposed Pan-EU pasteurization mandate. Centuries of safe production demonstrate that age-controlled raw-milk cheeses meet — and frequently exceed — public-health objectives. Position paper co-authored with Slow Food International and the French Conseil National des Appellations d'Origine Laitières.

United States · Congressional

Small-Producer Inspection Modernization Act (HR 4128 / S 2387)

Endorse

The federation endorses the bipartisan US bill (Rep. Pingree [D-ME] / Sen. Klobuchar [D-MN] / Sen. Tillis [R-NC]) modernizing FDA inspection protocols for sub-100,000-lb annual dairy operations. The bill creates a risk-based tier with periodic remote audits rather than the existing one-size-fits-all inspection cadence — appropriate for the small-producer reality.

Canada · CFIA

CFIA Aging Cave Inspection Frequency Reduction

Oppose

The federation opposes CFIA's proposed reduction in routine aging-cave inspection frequency for sub-50,000-kg producers from quarterly to annually. While we generally support risk-based regulatory simplification (see HR 4128), aging-cave conditions are sensitive enough that quarterly oversight provides a meaningful safety floor without imposing real burden. Position adopted after a member-producer survey (n=84) showed strong support for retention.

Codex Alimentarius

Codex CX/MMP 27/1 — Terroir-of-Origin Designation Harmonization

Monitor & Comment

The federation is monitoring the proposed Codex Alimentarius work item to harmonize terroir-of-origin designations across member states. We have not yet adopted a formal position; the European Standards Summit (October 22, 2026, Annecy) will produce a preliminary position for committee review.
