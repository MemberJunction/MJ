What: A ⌘K command palette is the universal entry point. Selecting text shows a popover (Quote / Add to stack); quotes pile into a reorderable stack above the composer. A persistent right rail is the auto-TOC — forks appear inline as child entries. Every action has a single-key binding; ? opens the full cheat-sheet.

Why world-class: mirrors Linear / Superhuman / Slack — keyboard-first operators never leave the home row, multi-quote lets you synthesize across several agent answers in one reply, and the discoverable palette means nothing is hidden behind muscle memory.

Tradeoff: dense and chrome-heavy; the always-on rail costs horizontal space and the shortcut surface can overwhelm casual users until the palette is learned.

What: Minimal chrome until you need it. A soft toolbar fades in on message hover (Quote / Fork / Copy) with text labels, not just icons. An inline hint teaches the select-to-quote gesture. Long threads surface a single friendly "Jump to" chip — a plain dropdown of section names. Forking is a warm, plain-English card, never jargon.

Why world-class: this is the iMessage / Notion school — power features stay invisible until contextually relevant, every control is self-describing, and there is essentially nothing to learn. First-time users succeed without a tutorial.

Tradeoff: mouse-forward and slower for heavy users — no keyboard acceleration, hover affordances are hard on touch, and the single jump chip won't scale to very deep threads the way a full rail would.

What: The organizing idea is a single left-hand "Map" that fuses the auto-TOC and the fork tree — sections and branches share one outline. A breadcrumb shows the current branch path; the reading pane is anchored by section headers. Quoting text routes the snippet into the active branch and the map updates live. Shortcuts (J/K navigate, Q quote, F branch) operate on the navigator.

Why world-class: treats a long, forked conversation like a document with a structure — the model people already hold for Git branches and outline editors. You always know where you are and how branches relate, which is exactly what breaks down in flat chat once a thread gets long.

Tradeoff: the heaviest mental model and the most screen real estate — overkill for short, single-line chats, and the tree needs careful design to avoid becoming cluttered as branches multiply.
