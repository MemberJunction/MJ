Design notes — Option C

Spreadsheet density. Built around the bulk-merge workflow: TagHealthJob spits out 60 high-confidence merge candidates after every run, and the reviewer needs to approve them all in 30 seconds, not 30 minutes. Multi-select + "Merge each into its best match" is the headline action.

  • Strengths: highest information density; spreadsheet keyboard ergonomics (⇧-click range, ⌘-A all); easy to sort by score and bulk-approve the top 90%; columns are user-configurable.
  • Weaknesses: intimidating for new users; reading-pane real estate is squeezed; doesn't communicate context as warmly as A; less visual.
  • Best for: daily admin clearing the merge backlog; data ops people exporting to CSV.
  • Distinctive: sticky bulk-action bar; inline confidence bar; "Select all 68 matching filter" cross-page select; per-row hover-actions.
