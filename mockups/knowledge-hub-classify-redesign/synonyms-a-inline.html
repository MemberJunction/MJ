Design notes — Option A

Synonyms live on the tag form itself, in their own tab. The pending-bar at the top forces LLM-suggested synonyms through human approval — they're shown in a distinct yellow row style. Source pill is a colored chip so you can scan a list of dozens and see "where did this come from?" at a glance. The hit-log panel below shows synonyms doing their job — a confidence-builder for skeptical admins.

  • Strengths: low-friction; lives in the natural place (the tag); pending-review channel keeps LLM auto-additions from polluting the active set; hit-log proves the value.
  • Weaknesses: hard to scan synonyms across all tags (need a separate page for that); 50+ synonyms on one tag would scroll forever.
  • Best for: typical 2–10 synonyms per tag; reviewing LLM auto-suggestions in context.
  • Distinctive: colored source-pill taxonomy; pending-bar yellow strip; hit-log panel below for trust-building.

B and C variants (described in index, not built): B (dedicated page) would aggregate all synonyms across all tags into one searchable page — useful for de-dup audits. C (chip stack with bulk paste) would feature a "paste a CSV" dialog for importing 100s of synonyms at once.
