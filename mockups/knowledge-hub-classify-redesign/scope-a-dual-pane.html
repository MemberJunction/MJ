Design notes — Option A

Bulletproof transfer-list pattern. The middle column of arrow buttons (single + bulk) is the workhorse; keyboard users can ⌘-A on the available pane and bulk-add. Greyed-out rows on the left show why they can't be added (parent scope conflict), turning a validation rule into a visible explanation.

  • Strengths: visually clear "before / after"; explicit move semantics; failure modes (scope conflict) explained inline; affected-children preview makes inheritance visible.
  • Weaknesses: wide layout (3 panes); slow for picking 1 of 1000 tenants; mobile-hostile.
  • Best for: typical 5–50 tenant scopes; one-shot setup of new tags.
  • Distinctive: middle arrow column; disabled-row inline reasoning; affected-children inheritance preview as a panel below.

B and C variants — described in index, not yet built: B (chip multi-picker) would be a single-row autocomplete with chips for selected scopes — compact, modern, but harder for 50+ scopes. C (Tag × Tenant matrix) would be a heatmap of every tag vs. every tenant with cells colored by scope membership — great for audits, terrible for editing one tag.
