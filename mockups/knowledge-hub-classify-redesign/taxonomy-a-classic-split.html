Design notes — Option A

The reference design. Tree-on-left mirrors the existing autotagging-pipeline component, so adoption cost is near zero. The form on the right collapses governance (toggles), scope (chip list), and synonyms (chip list) into one scrollable surface, with a stats strip at the top so reviewers see "is this tag healthy?" before editing. The tree icons (🌐 global, 🔒 scoped, ❄ frozen, 🚫 no-grow, ! requires-review, ⚠ wide-node) carry a lot of meaning in tiny pixels — they're the most distinctive visual element.

  • Strengths: familiar, scannable, governance fits naturally in the form, tree icons make scope/freeze instantly visible.
  • Weaknesses: bulk operations across nodes require multi-select in the tree (which the icon legend mostly precludes); not great for "show me every frozen tag in the org".
  • Best for: day-to-day curation by a single owner per subtree.
  • Distinctive: icon legend on the tree; toggle-row with inline description; stats strip pinned above the form.
