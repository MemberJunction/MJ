Design notes — Option B

Visual prioritization at a glance. Each Reason gets its own column so triage by category becomes a glance, not a filter click. Drag-and-drop to drop zones automates the most common dispositions; clicking a card opens the detail drawer (same as Option A's reading pane).

  • Strengths: queue health is visible immediately (column heights = problem distribution); social proof — drag feels gratifying; matches reviewer's mental model of "I'm clearing the merge column today".
  • Weaknesses: 7+ columns is a lot; horizontal scrolling on smaller screens; drag UX needs careful keyboard fallback; harder to surface deep details inline.
  • Best for: daily triage by an admin who works across all reasons; team leads who want to assign columns to junior reviewers.
  • Distinctive: reason-as-column model; left-border priority bars (high-confidence merges flagged); drop-zone tray at the bottom replaces row-level buttons.
