Design notes — Option A

The most familiar pattern for queue work: 3-pane email layout with keyboard-first navigation. Optimized for careful single-item review of the long tail ("Below threshold" and "Requires review" reasons). The threshold ribbon visualization is the signature element — it makes the score-band ambiguity visceral.

  • Strengths: instant familiarity, full context per item, keyboard-driven, easy mental model.
  • Weaknesses: bulk operations feel awkward (have to multi-select in left list); 100-item merge approval is slow.
  • Best for: first-time reviewers; "Requires review" items where stakes are high.
  • Distinctive: per-item threshold-band ribbon; closest-5 candidate cards inline; exact "what will this approval cost" preview.
